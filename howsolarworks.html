Solar panels

A solar panel is made of numerous small photovaltaic cells (photovaltaic means they convert sunlight into energy). These cells alow light particles known as photons to knock electrons away from atoms, creating a flow of electricity. Each of these cells is made of "slices" of semi-conducting metal. Usually these parts are made of silicon. To work, photovoltaic cells need to establish an electric field. An electric field occurs when opposite charges are separated. To get this field, manufacturers "dope" silicon with other materials, giving each half of the cell a positive or negative electrical charge.

Charge controller

The charge controller acts as a regulator. It delivers power from the PV array to system loads and the battery bank. When the battery bank is nearly full, the controller will taper off the charging current to maintain the required voltage to fully charge the battery and keep it topped off. By being able to regulate the voltage, the solar controller protects the battery. The key word is “protects.” Batteries can be the most expensive part of a system, and a solar charge controller protects them from both overcharging and undercharging.

Battery system

Solar batteries work by storing energy produced by your solar panels for later use. When you install a solar battery as part of your solar panel system, you are able to store excess solar electricity at your home instead of sending it back to the grid. If your solar panels are producing more electricity than you need, the excess energy goes towards charging the battery. When your solar panels aren’t producing electricity, for example at night, it will be drawn from the battery.

DC power

DC stands for Direct current. It is the one directional or unidirectional flow of electric charge. An electrochemical cell is a prime example of DC power. Direct current may flow through a conductor such as a wire, but can also flow through semiconductors, insulators, or even through a vacuum. The electric current flows in a constant direction, distinguishing it from alternating current (AC). this type of current was once known as galvanic current.

Inverter

A solar inverter or PV inverter, is a type of electrical converter which takes the direct current output of a photovoltaic solar panel and converts it into a utility frequency alternating current that can be fed into a commercial electrical grid or used by a local, off-grid electrical network. It is a critical balance of system–component in a photovoltaic system, allowing the use of ordinary AC-powered equipment. Solar power inverters have special functions adapted for use with photovoltaic arrays, including maximum power point tracking and anti-islanding protection.

AC power

Unlike direct current, which travels in one direction only, alternating current (AC) changes its magnitude continuously. Electrical power is delivered to businesses and residences in the form of alternating current and it is commonly used when kitchen apliances or a television is plugged into a wall socket. since the electrical power that comes from batteries and battery cells is delivered in the form of direct current (DC), an inverter is needed to change the electrical power into AC.
